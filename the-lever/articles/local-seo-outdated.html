Everything you read about local SEO in 2024 is now wrong. Here’s the updated playbook.

Google’s local algorithm shifted. NAP citations, keyword-stuffed GMB posts, and the old playbook are dead. Here’s what actually works now.

R Reese
March 7, 2026 Issue No. 009

I’m going to say something that’s going to annoy a lot of SEO consultants: most of the local SEO advice published in 2024 is now actively hurting the businesses that follow it.

Not “slightly outdated.” Not “needs a refresh.” Wrong. As in: doing the opposite of what Google’s local algorithm now rewards.

I’ve been tracking the local search algorithm through 34 businesses we work with since late 2025. What I’ve seen in the data over the last four months has been a quiet, systematic demolition of the old playbook. Here’s what changed and what to do about it.

What died: the NAP citation factory

For years, the gospel of local SEO was: get your Name, Address, and Phone number listed on as many directories as possible. Yelp. Yellow Pages. Foursquare. Random industry directories nobody visits. The theory was that citation volume signalled legitimacy to Google.

0
ranking improvement from 40+ new citations (our test)

We tested this directly. Took four businesses with no directory presence beyond the basics and submitted them to 40+ directories over two months. Net ranking change? Zero. Not a single position gained in local pack results. The citation-volume signal has been functionally deprecated.

Meanwhile, a pizza shop that did nothing except respond to every Google review within 2 hours moved from position 14 to the Map Pack in the same timeframe.

Google stopped caring how many places list your phone number. It started caring how many people interact with your listing and what happens when they do.

What died: keyword-stuffed GBP posts

You know those Google Business Profile posts that read like a robot having a stroke? “Best pizza restaurant in downtown Portland Oregon serving authentic Italian pizza with fresh mozzarella cheese for lunch and dinner.”

They used to work. Kind of. In 2024, you could stuff keywords into GBP posts and Google would occasionally reward you with a relevance boost.

That’s over. Google’s March 2025 local update introduced what appears to be a content-quality filter for GBP posts. We watched three businesses that were heavy keyword-stuffers actually drop in rankings after the update. The signal flipped.

What works now: the new local signals

Here’s the updated playbook, based on what we’re actually seeing move rankings across 34 businesses in 2026.

1. Review velocity beats review volume.

It’s not about having 500 reviews anymore. It’s about how fast you’re getting new ones. A business with 80 reviews gaining 12 per month will outrank a business with 400 reviews gaining 2 per month. Google is weighting recency and velocity over raw count.

12
reviews per month moved a clinic into the Map Pack

We helped a physiotherapy clinic implement automated review requests — a text sent 2 hours after each appointment. They went from 3 reviews per month to 12. Within 8 weeks, they moved from position 7 to the local 3-pack. Their total review count was still lower than four competitors above them. Velocity won.

2. Behavioural signals are the new backlinks.

Google is now heavily weighting what users do after they find your listing. Do they click for directions? Do they call? Do they visit your website and stay? Or do they bounce back to the search results and click the next business?

Your Google listing isn’t a billboard anymore. It’s a conversion page. And Google is grading you on conversion rate.

This means your GBP listing needs to do real work. Complete services list. Accurate hours. Photos that are actually of your business (not stock photos — Google can tell). A description that makes someone click “directions” instead of scrolling to the next result.

One restaurant we work with replaced their 6 stock photos with 22 real photos of their food, interior, and team. Click-to-call rate increased 34%. Direction requests increased 28%. They gained two local pack positions in 5 weeks without changing anything else.

3. AI-structured content on your website.

Google’s local algorithm now pulls information from your website to answer questions directly in search results. If your website content is structured in a way that’s easy for AI to parse — clear headings, FAQ sections with schema markup, service pages with specific details — Google will feature you more prominently.

This isn’t traditional “SEO content.” It’s closer to building a knowledge base about your business that Google’s AI can read and reference. Think: specific service descriptions with pricing ranges, detailed FAQ pages that answer real customer questions, and location pages with genuine neighbourhood context.

4. GBP activity patterns matter.

Google is tracking how actively you manage your Business Profile. Not just posts — everything. Review responses. Photo uploads. Q&A answers. Menu updates. Hour changes. Businesses that show consistent, ongoing activity on their GBP get a measurable boost.

34
businesses we tracked through these algorithm changes

The cadence that works best from our data: respond to every review within 24 hours, add 2–3 new photos per week, post one genuine update per week (not keyword-stuffed — something a real customer would find useful), and update your services or menu at least monthly.

The bottom line

The old local SEO playbook was about gaming signals. Build citations. Stuff keywords. Accumulate reviews. It was a checklist you could hand to a virtual assistant and forget about.

The new playbook is about genuine engagement. Earn reviews consistently. Make your listing so good that people act on it. Structure your web content for AI comprehension. Show Google you’re actively running a real business.

That’s harder to fake. Which is exactly the point. Google got better at detecting the difference between businesses that game the system and businesses that actually serve their customers well. The algorithm finally caught up with reality.

If you’re still running the 2024 playbook, you’re not just wasting time. You might be actively hurting your rankings. The rules changed. Time to update the playbook.
