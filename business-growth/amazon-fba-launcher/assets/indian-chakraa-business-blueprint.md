### 2.3 Durability Engineering

**Stone Attachment Protocol (Double-Lock System):**
1. Each stone chip is wire-wrapped with a minimum 2-turn mechanical crimp
2. A micro-dot of industrial-grade cyanoacrylate (super glue) is applied at the crimp junction
3. This two-layer system means even if glue fails, the mechanical crimp holds

**Shake Test Standard:** Product must survive a 30-second moderate shake without any stone detachment. Reject any unit that fails.

## SECTION 7 — EXPORT FROM INDIA

### 7.1 Export Registration Requirements

| Requirement | Details | Cost | Timeline |
|-------------|---------|------|----------|
| **IEC (Import Export Code)** | Apply via DGFT online portal (dgft.gov.in); PAN-based; lifetime validity | Free (₹0) | 3–5 business days |
| **GST Registration** | Required for claiming input tax credits on exports; LUT filing for zero-rated export | ₹0 (if already registered) | Already active or 7–10 days |
| **AD Code Registration** | Register your Authorized Dealer code with your bank for foreign exchange | Free | 2–3 days at your bank |
| **RCMC (optional)** | Registration-Cum-Membership Certificate from Export Promotion Council; enables certain benefits | ₹5,000–₹10,000 | 7–14 days |

### 7.2 HS Codes

| Product | Likely HS Code | Description | US Duty Rate |
|---------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Crystal tree (semi-precious) | 7116.20.00 | Articles of semi-precious stones | 0–6.5% |
| Card deck (printed) | 4901.99.00 | Other printed matter | 0% |
| Packaging (rigid box) | 4819.20.00 | Folding cartons, boxes | 0–1.5% |

**Important:** Confirm HS codes with your customs broker before first shipment. Misclassification can cause customs delays and penalties.

### 7.3 Export Documentation Checklist

1. Commercial Invoice (with buyer/seller details, HS codes, FOB value)
2. Packing List (unit count, weight, dimensions per carton)
3. Bill of Lading (from freight forwarder/shipping line)
4. Shipping Bill (filed via ICEGATE by customs broker)
5. Letter of Undertaking (LUT) for GST-free export
6. Certificate of Origin (if claiming preferential duty rates)
7. Insurance Certificate (marine cargo insurance)

### 7.4 Freight Options Comparison

| Incoterm | What's Included | Your Responsibility | Best For |
|----------|----------------|--------------------|---------|
| **EXW (Ex Works)** | Product at your factory door | Everything: inland freight, export customs, ocean freight, US import, delivery | Not recommended — too much complexity for a beginner |
| **FOB (Free On Board)** | Product loaded onto vessel at Indian port | US-side: ocean freight, customs, delivery to FBA | Good if you have a US-side freight partner |
| **DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)** | Full door-to-door delivery to Amazon FBA warehouse | Nothing — supplier/forwarder handles everything | **Recommended for beginners** |

**Recommendation:** Use **DDP via an experienced freight forwarder** for your first 2–3 shipments. This eliminates the complexity of US customs brokerage. Recommended forwarders for India→US Amazon FBA: FreightPop, Freightos, or specialized FBA prep services found on IndiaMart/Alibaba.

**Estimated DDP cost for 500 units (LCL):** $1,200–$1,800 total ($2.40–$3.60 per unit).

### 7.5 GST on Exports

Exports from India are **zero-rated** under GST. Two options:
1. **LUT Route (recommended):** File a Letter of Undertaking with GST portal → export without paying IGST → no refund process needed
2. **IGST Route:** Pay IGST on export → claim refund later (ties up working capital for 2–3 months)

File LUT annually on the GST portal before your first export of each financial year.

## SECTION 8 — US COMPLIANCE

### 8.1 Regulatory Assessment

| Regulation | Applicable? | Reasoning |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **CPSIA (Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act)** | Partially — only if marketed to children under 12 | Crystal trees are NOT children's products if marketed as adult décor/spiritual items. **Do NOT market to children.** If positioned as adult spiritual décor, CPSIA testing is not required. |
| **California Prop 65** | Yes — likely required | Natural stones may contain trace minerals (e.g., lead in lapis lazuli, crystalline silica). Add Prop 65 warning label to be safe. Cost: ~$0.02/unit for a label. |
| **ASTM F963** | No | Only applies to toys. This is adult décor. |
| **FCC** | No | No electronic components. |
| **FDA** | No | Not a food, drug, or cosmetic. Do NOT make health claims. |

### 8.2 Required Product Labeling

| Label Element | Requirement | Implementation |
|--------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Country of Origin** | Required by US CBP (19 CFR 134) | "Made in India" — printed on product base sticker, on packaging, and on Amazon listing |
| **Material Description** | Best practice for consumer trust | "Natural semi-precious gemstones, copper wire, natural agate base" — on packaging |
| **Prop 65 Warning** | Required for California sales | Standard Prop 65 warning label on packaging: "⚠ WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [substance], which is known to the State of California to cause [cancer/reproductive harm]." |
| **Choking Hazard** | Required IF small parts could detach AND product is for children | Add "Not intended for children under 14" on packaging. If stones are securely attached and product is marketed to adults, formal choking hazard label is not legally required — but adding it provides extra liability protection. |
| **UPC/EAN Barcode** | Required for Amazon FBA | GS1-issued barcode on outer packaging |
| **FNSKU Label** | Required for Amazon FBA commingled inventory opt-out | Amazon-generated label on each unit |

### 8.3 Critical Compliance Rule

**Do NOT make medical or health claims.** Phrases like "heals anxiety," "cures pain," or "treats depression" can trigger FDA action. Safe language: "designed for meditation and spiritual practice," "associated with traditional chakra philosophy," "may promote a sense of calm" (with disclaimer).

## SECTION 14 — RISK ANALYSIS

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Crystals fall off during shipping** | Medium | High — returns + bad reviews | Double-lock attachment + foam packaging + shake test QC |
| **Shipping delays (ocean freight)** | Medium | Medium — delays launch by 2–4 weeks | Build 3-week buffer into timeline; have backup air freight quote ready |
| **Amazon listing suppressed** | Low | High — blocks all revenue | Pre-check compliance; avoid health claims; ensure proper labeling |
| **Copycat competitors** | High (6–12 months) | Medium — price pressure | Card deck + brand story + packaging create 6–12 month copy barrier; file design patents if possible |
| **Prop 65 complaint** | Low-Medium | Medium — forced label addition | Include Prop 65 label from day 1 (costs $0.02/unit — just do it) |
| **Negative early reviews** | Medium | High — tanks conversion rate | Vine program for early controlled reviews; obsessive QC; responsive customer service |
| **Currency fluctuation (INR/USD)** | Medium | Low-Medium — margin impact | Price product with 5% margin buffer; hedge via forward contracts at scale |
| **Customs classification dispute** | Low | Medium — delays + penalties | Pre-classify with customs broker; have HS code documentation ready |
| **Supplier quality inconsistency** | Medium | High — batch-to-batch variation | Written QC spec document; third-party inspection before each shipment |
| **Trademark rejection** | Low-Medium | Medium — delays Brand Registry | Conduct thorough pre-filing search; consider attorney review ($200–$400) |

### Risk-Adjusted Decision Matrix

**Top 3 risks to actively manage:**
1. Product quality (solve with QC protocol + shake test standard)
2. Copycat competition (solve with card deck + brand story + packaging moat)
3. Negative early reviews (solve with Vine + obsessive QC + premium packaging)
